Tuition Tracker is an interactive tool designed to help parents and students find the “right” college or university among thousands by filtering through desired search criteria, such as location, price and size.

Tuition Tracker is also a resource for education researchers and journalists looking to explore trends in tuition, financial aid and student outcomes among U.S. higher education over the last decade.

Tuition Tracker is the result of a collaboration between The Dallas Morning News, Education Writers Association and The Hechinger Report.

DATA & METHODOLOGY:

Tuition Tracker is powered by data provided by the U.S. Department of Education from IPEDS, the Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System. The institutions analyzed are all U.S.-based degree-granting colleges and universities that have first-time, full-time undergraduates. The 2006-07 to 2016-17 data releases were used to track and chart the following:

  • Tuition (net price and total cost of attendance)
  • Financial aid (grants, including Pell, and federal loans)
  • Enrollment (by race and gender)
  • Graduation rates (by race)
  • Time to degree
  • Retention rates

Data from 2017-18 were omitted because the release was provisional at the time of publication.

While most of the data comes directly from IPEDS, several data points were calculated from other IPEDS information. These include:

  • Percentage of students paying net price by family income bracket

    This data point isn't directly available in IPEDS, but can be calculated by adding together the total number of students in each income bracket and dividing it by the number of students in each income bracket. This number is converted to a percentage by multiplying by 100.


  • Enrollment by race

    IPEDS data rounds enrollement data to the nearest whole number. This means that any student population less than 1 percent appears as if no students are that race. Instead of using the derived IPEDS variable, this data point was calculated in order to give a more exact count of an institution's undergraduates. Enrollment by race is calculated by dividing the total number of students of a race/ethnicity by the total number of undergraduates and rounding to the nearest tenth.
